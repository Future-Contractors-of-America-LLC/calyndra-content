# Caregiver Onboarding

Welcome. You are about to use **Calyndra**—a calm AAC guidance system—and **Caly**, its speak-only companion. This guide gets you started in ten minutes without pressure.

## Before you begin

1. **Read the boundaries.** Caly offers AAC and caregiver guidance. Caly does not diagnose, prescribe, treat, or respond to emergencies. Keep your clinician team in the loop.
2. **Choose your audience mode.** Start in **Caregiver** mode unless you are previewing what a learner will hear (toddler, child, teen, adult).
3. **Sit together.** When a child uses the screen, stay beside them. Your voice matters more than the app.

## Your first session (10 minutes)

### Step 1 — Pick one core word

Open **Vocabulary** or the **Symbol grid** and choose one word:

- **help** — when something is hard
- **more** — when they want to continue
- **stop** — when they need a pause (honor it immediately)

Tap the symbol. Caly will suggest how to **model** the word in everyday life—not as a test, but as a gentle offer.

### Step 2 — Model in real life

Within the next hour, use the word once in context:

- Hold the symbol or say the word yourself
- Wait 5–10 seconds without demanding a response
- If they reach, point, or vocalize—celebrate the attempt, not perfection

### Step 3 — Save what helps

If Caly’s reply feels useful, tap **Save to favorites**. Favorites stay **on your device only**—nothing is uploaded to a learner profile.

### Step 4 — Ask Caly a real question

In **Chat**, try one of these:

- "How do I introduce bathroom without shame?"
- "My child says stop during meals—what should I do?"
- "What is assent-aware modeling for a tired toddler?"

Caly answers in plain language. You decide what fits your family or classroom.

## Age modes explained

| Mode | Best for | What changes |
|------|----------|--------------|
| Caregiver | You | Full guidance, safety notes, practical steps |
| Toddler (2–4) | Preview + read-aloud | Very short sentences; large symbols |
| Child (5–12) | School-age preview | Encouraging, assent-aware tone |
| Teen (13–17) | Adolescent preview | Direct, respectful, no condescension |
| Adult (18+) | Self-advocate preview | Clear boundaries, clinical disclaimers |

Switch modes anytime from the toolbar (web) or Settings (mobile).

## Privacy you should know

- **On device:** Last 20 chat messages and favorites live in local storage. Clear them by removing favorites or clearing browser/app data.
- **When you send a message:** Text goes to our Azure API and Azure OpenAI to generate a reply. We do **not** sell data or build advertising profiles.
- **On server:** We log metadata only (audience, length, outcome)—not the message body—in an audit table.

Full details: see the Calyndra **Privacy Policy** (`privacy.html` on web).

## When to get professional help

Contact your speech-language pathologist, physician, or educator when:

- Swallowing, breathing, or acute pain is involved
- You need a formal AAC device assessment
- Behavior signals distress you cannot safely support alone

**Emergency:** Call **911** (or your local emergency number). Caly cannot dispatch help.

## Pace and assent

There is no deadline. One symbol per week is progress. If the learner says **no** or **stop**, pause without punishment. Trust builds faster than drills.
