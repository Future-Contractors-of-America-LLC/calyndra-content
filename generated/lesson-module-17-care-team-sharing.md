# Sharing Calyndra with your care team

Session summaries and routine boards in Calyndra are **transparency tools** — they help families describe what happened at home. They are not diagnosis evidence, treatment outcomes, or a substitute for clinical documentation.

## What to share
- Which symbols your child taps most often
- Routine steps completed (or skipped — skips count as assent)
- Play games tried and caregiver modeling notes
- Sensory settings that helped today

## What not to claim
- That completion equals mastery or clinical progress
- That Calyndra data replaces SLP/OT/BCBA notes
- That the app diagnosed or treated anything

## How to introduce Calyndra
1. **One sentence:** "We use Calyndra for picture words and visual routines at home — assent-first, skippable."
2. **Offer a demo:** Show one symbol tap or one first/then board on your phone.
3. **Invite feedback:** Ask whether vocabulary or routine order should align with school or clinic goals.

## Caly in caregiver mode
Caregiver mode uses the same Caly (Canopy) plant friend and adult voice base with a warm professional tone. Educational support only — not medical advice.
