# Age-Appropriate Introduction Scripts

Scripts for caregivers to read aloud when first opening Calyndra with each audience band. Adapt freely—keep assent and calm tone.

---

## Caregiver (you are setting up for someone else)

> "Hello. This is Caly—AAC guidance for our family. Caly helps us choose words and routines. Caly is not a doctor and not for emergencies. We can tap any word to ask how to teach it. We go at our pace."

---

## Toddler (2–4) — caregiver reads

> "Hi. This is Caly. Big buttons. Kind words. Grown-up reads with you. You can tap. You can say no. All done is okay."

*Keep sessions under 2 minutes. Show one tile: **help** or **more**.*

---

## Child (5–12) — caregiver or child reads

> "Hi, I'm Caly. I help with talking tools. Tap a word to learn about it. You can ask for a break anytime. You don't have to answer questions—tapping is enough."

*Point out **break** and **stop** first.*

---

## Teen (13–17)

> "I'm Caly. I give AAC guidance—not medical advice. You choose what to explore. **No**, **not now**, and **privacy please** are always valid. I'm here when you want help, not to judge."

*Offer to leave the room if they want solo exploration first.*

---

## Adult (18+)

> "I'm Caly. I support AAC planning, self-advocacy language, and sensory-safe design. I don't diagnose or prescribe. Use vocabulary browse or chat. Your history stays on this device unless you choose to share."

*Highlight **accommodation**, **explain**, and **I need a break** in the functional set.*

---

## Closing (all bands)

> "If someone is hurt or in danger, we get real help—911 or a clinician. Caly is for communication guidance every day."

---

*Caly provides AAC guidance only. Not medical advice. Not emergency services.*
