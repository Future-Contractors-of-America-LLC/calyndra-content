# Sage's Forgiveness Trail (teen, ~30 min, 300 scenes)

**Series:** Caly and Friends  
**Band:** Teen (Caly Vine, 13-17)  
**Friend:** Sage the deer  
**Mentor:** Elder Oak  
**Christian lesson:** Forgiveness frees you from carrying old hurt. (Matthew 6:14 - if you forgive others, your Father will forgive you).

## Story arc

| Act | Scenes | Beats |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Old wound | 1-45 | Sage remembers Reed's harsh words months ago |
| Heavy pack | 46-90 | Metaphor: Sage carries heavy stone in pack |
| Trail start | 91-135 | Elder Oak sends Sage on forgiveness trail |
| Stones down | 136-180 | At each marker, Sage sets down a stone |
| Reed meeting | 181-225 | Reed apologizes sincerely, Sage forgives |
| Light pack | 226-300 | Sage finishes trail unburdened, helps others |

## Key scenes

| # | Visual | Dialogue |
|---|--------|----------|
| 1 | Sage on trail with visibly heavy backpack | I've carried this hurt for too long. |
| 2 | Each trail marker is a stone cairn | Forgiveness is a path, not a single step. |
| 3 | Elder Oak at trailhead, gentle voice | Put down what weighs you down. Travel lighter. |
| 4 | Sage places stone at marker one: the anger | I release this. It doesn't serve me anymore. |
| 5 | Reed waits at trail midpoint, head bowed | I was wrong. I'm sorry. Will you forgive me? |
| 6 | Sage and Reed walk final segment together | Forgiveness doesn't forget. It frees. |
| 7 | Sage's pack glows light at trail end | I am free. I can help others on this path too. |
