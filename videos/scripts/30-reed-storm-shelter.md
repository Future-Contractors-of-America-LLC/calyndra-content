# Reed's Storm Shelter (tween, ~20 min, 200 scenes)

**Series:** Caly and Friends  
**Band:** Tween (Caly Sprig, 9-12)  
**Friend:** Reed the owl  
**Mentor:** Elder Oak  
**Christian lesson:** Storms pass faster when friends shelter together. (Psalm 46:1 - God is our refuge and strength).

## Story arc

| Act | Scenes | Beats |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Storm warning | 1-30 | Dark clouds, wind rises, friends scatter scared |
| Shelter call | 31-70 | Reed calls all to Elder Oak hollow |
| Inside together | 71-110 | Share blankets, stories, calm breathing |
| Storm peak | 111-150 | Loudest thunder, group hug, song |
| Clear sky | 151-200 | Storm ends, rainbow, thank you circle |

## Key scenes

| # | Visual | Dialogue |
|---|--------|----------|
| 1 | Dark clouds rolling, wind bending flowers | A big storm is coming. Everyone find shelter! |
| 2 | Reed circles above, calling friends below | Follow me to Elder Oak. We'll be safe together. |
| 3 | Inside hollow trunk, friends huddle close | Close together. Breathe slow. You're not alone. |
| 4 | Thunder crack, Pip trembles, Moss holds paw | Storms are loud, but they don't last forever. |
| 5 | Elder Oak trunk solid against wind | God is our shelter when the world feels wild. |
| 6 | Reed leads a quiet hum, hearts steady | We weathered it because we stayed together. |
| 7 | Rain stops, rainbow over meadow, friends emerge | The storm passed. We are stronger together. |
