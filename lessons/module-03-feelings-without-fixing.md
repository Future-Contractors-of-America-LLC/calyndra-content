# Module 3: Feelings Without Fixing

**Time:** 8 minutes, 4 days per week  
**Goal:** Introduce **happy**, **sad**, and **mad** as name-it words—not problems to solve

## Principle
Naming a feeling is communication success. You do not need to fix the feeling immediately.

## Activity — Feelings walk
Walk through the home. When you notice an expression:
- "You look happy." ? model **happy**
- "You seem sad." ? model **sad**
- "You look mad." ? model **mad**

Do **not** ask "Are you sad?" as a quiz. Narrate gently once.

## Sensory-safe presentation
- Muted tile colors (no bright red alarm faces for **mad**)
- Simple text labels
- Caregiver voice stays calm and low

## Boundaries
- **hurt** and **sick** are not the same as **sad**. If pain or illness is suspected, contact a clinician—not Caly.
- Caly does not interpret medical symptoms.

## Ask Caly
*"How do I respond when my child taps sad but won't explain why?"*
