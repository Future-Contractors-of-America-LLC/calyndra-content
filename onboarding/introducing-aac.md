# How to Introduce AAC (Caly's Guide for Caregivers)

Introducing AAC works best when it feels like a natural part of life—not a test.

## Before you begin

- **Choose a calm moment.** Not during a meltdown or rush.
- **Start with one symbol** the person already uses (gesture, word, or picture).
- **Model without demanding.** You tap or say the word; they watch. No "say it back" pressure.

## Step-by-step (first two weeks)

### Week 1: Model only
Pick 3–5 core words from the toddler or child set: **help**, **more**, **all done**, **eat**, **stop**.

Each day, model each word **3–5 times** in real contexts:
- At snack: tap **more** when offering another bite.
- Leaving the park: tap **all done** before you go.
- When they reach for something: model **help** while assisting.

Wait **5–10 seconds** after you model. Silence is okay.

### Week 2: Offer access
Place the board or app where they can reach it. If they tap a symbol—even accidentally—honor it when safe:
- Tap **drink** ? offer water.
- Tap **stop** ? pause the activity.

## Common mistakes (and kinder alternatives)

| Instead of… | Try… |
|-------------|------|
| "Use your words!" | Model the symbol calmly once |
| Taking the device when they tap **no** | Respect **no**; offer a different choice |
| Adding 20 symbols at once | Add one new symbol per week |
| Quizzing ("What is this?") | Narrating ("You tapped **bathroom**. Let's go.") |

## When to seek a professional

Speech-language pathologists and AAC specialists can personalize vocabulary and access methods. Caly supports your learning—I do not replace their clinical judgment.

## Assent reminder

If the person turns away, pushes the device, or says **stop**, that is communication. Pause. Try again later—or not at all today.
